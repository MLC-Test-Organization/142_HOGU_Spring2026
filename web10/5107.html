    2.

    Consider the function \(f(x)=-\frac{1}{4}x^2+1\text{.}\)
    🔗
    (a)
    Sketch a graph of \(f(x)\) and use your sketch to draw the rectangles that represent the right-hand Riemann sum on \([-2,0]\) using \(4\) subintervals of equal width.
    🔗
    🔗
    (b)
    Estimate the area under the curve of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([-2,0]\) using a right-hand Riemann sum with \(4\) subintervals of equal width. Is this an overestimate or an underestimate?
    🔗
    🔗
    (c)
    State the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus Part 2 (see page 709 of your textbook).
    🔗
    🔗
    (d)
    Use the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus Part 2 to find the exact value of \(\int_{-2}^0 f(x)\,dx\text{.}\) How does this compare to your answer in part (b)?
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    5.

    A local bagel shop’s marginal profit is given by \(p^\prime(x)=0.05x^2-2.1x+100\) dollars per bagel when \(x\) bagels are sold.
    🔗
    (a)
    Estimate the change in profit when the number of bagels increases from \(50\) to \(59\) using \(3\) subintervals of equal width and a left Riemann sum.
    🔗
    🔗
    (b)
    Find the exact change in profit when the number of bagels increases from \(50\) to \(59\) using the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus Part 2.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    6.

    A company sells \(x\) handbags per year priced at \(\$p\) per handbag. The company’s price-demand function for the handbags is given by \(p(x)=100-0.05x\text{.}\) Each handbag costs \(\$15\) to manufacture and the company has fixed costs of \(\$12{,}000\) per year.
    🔗
    (a)
    What is the yearly cost function \(C(x)\text{?}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    (b)
    What is the yearly revenue function \(R(x)\text{?}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    (c)
    Use parts (a) and (b) to determine the yearly profit function \(P(x)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    (d)
    Find a reasonable domain to restrict \(P(x)\) to.
    🔗
    🔗
    (e)
    How many handbags must the company sell to maximize its profit? What is the company’s maximum profit?
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    7.

    A manufacturer needs to make a closed cylindrical can that will hold \(1500\) cm3 of water. Determine the dimensions (in cm) of the can that will use the least amount of material.
    🔗
    (Hint: The formula for the volume of a cylinder is \(V=\pi r^2h\) and the formula for the surface area of a cylinder is \(A=2\pi rh+2\pi r^2\text{.}\))
    🔗
    (a)
    Is this a maximum or minimum problem?
    🔗
    🔗
    (b)
    What is to be maximized/minimized?
    🔗
    🔗
    (c)
    Formulate the objective function.
    🔗
    🔗
    (d)
    Formulate any constraint equations.
    🔗
    🔗
    (e)
    Find any critical values of the objective function.
    🔗
    🔗
    (f)
    Provide the final answer (with units).
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗
