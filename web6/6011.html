    7.

    Theorem 1.10.
    Suppose \(f(x)\) is continuous on an interval containing the critical value \(x = c\) and \(f(x)\) is differentiable near, and on both sides of, \(x = c\text{.}\) Then, \(f (c)\) satisfies one of the following:
    • If \(f '(x)\) changes sign from positive to negative at \(x = c\text{,}\) then \(f(c)\) is a local maximum of \(f(x)\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    • If \(f '(x)\) changes sign from negative to positive at \(x = c\text{,}\) then \(f (c)\) is a local minimum of \(f(x)\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    • If \(f'(x)\) does not change sign at \(x = c\text{,}\) then \(f (c)\) is neither a local maximum nor a local minimum of \(f(x)\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
