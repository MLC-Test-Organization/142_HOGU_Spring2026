    8.

    The product of two positive numbers, \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) is 100. Find \(x\) and \(y\) such that \(M\) is a minimum given \(\D M=2x+3y\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Follow the optimization process below (also in your notes) to solve the problem. Steps 1 and 3 are already done for you, but make sure you understand them!
    🔗
    1. Step 1. Translate the word problem into mathematical form using the following sub-steps:
      🔗
      1. Identify the quantity you want to maximize or minimize. Minimize \(M\) where \(M = 2x+3y\text{.}\)
        🔗
      2. Draw a picture (if applicable). Not Applicable.
        🔗
      3. State the objective function and any constraint equations. Objective function: \(M = 2x+3y\text{,}\) Constraint Equation: \(xy=100\text{.}\)
        🔗
      🔗
    2. Step 2. State the objective equation in terms of one variable.
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Step 3. Find the interval on which the objective function must be optimized. \((0,\infty)\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    4. Step 4. Use calculus to find the optimal solution by following the sub-steps below:
      🔗
      1. Find the critical values of the objective function.
        🔗
      2. If the interval is closed, evaluate the objective function at the critical values in the interval as well as the endpoints of the interval. If the interval is open and there is only one critical value in the interval, do the first or second derivative test.
        🔗
      🔗
    5. Step 5. Provide the final answer to the problem (include units if applicable).

    9.

    Your friend’s family is planning to build a rectangular swimming pool. The water surface is to have an area of 1200 square feet. The pool will also have a 6-foot-wide walkway on the east and west sides and an 8-foot walkway on the north and south sides. Let \(x\) be the length of the pool (east to west), and let \(y\) be the width of the pool (north to south). Given that \(\D 10 \leq x \leq 100\text{,}\) find the dimensions of the pool that will minimize the total area of the swimming pool and walkways.

    10.

    The Tool Shack manufactures ratchet sets. The monthly fixed costs are \$15,000 and variable costs are \$180 for each ratchet set produced. The company estimates that 300 sets can be sold each month if the unit price is \$280 and that 20 more units can be sold for each decrease of \$14 in the price. Let \(x\) represent the number of ratchet sets produced and sold each month.
    🔗
    (a)
    Determine the monthly cost function \(C(x)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    (b)
    Assuming the demand function follows a linear model, determine the monthly revenue function, \(R(x)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    (c)
    Determine the value of \(x\) that maximizes the monthly profit, assuming that \(150 \leq x \leq 580\text{.}\) Round to the nearest whole if necessary.
